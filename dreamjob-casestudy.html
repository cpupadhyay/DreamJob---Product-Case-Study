The Real Problem
Isn't Opportunity. It's Chaos.

"I missed an interview email because my notifications were off. By the time I saw it, they'd moved on."

User research interview — final-year student
📭
No Tracking System
Applications go to 4+ platforms. Status updates live in different inboxes. There's no single source of truth — so opportunities fall through the cracks silently.
🔕
Missed Recruiter Responses
Recruiters move fast. A reply sitting unread for 48 hours can mean the role is filled. Without smart alerts, freshers lose interviews they were qualified for.
🤝
Referrals Go Cold
A warm referral is the highest-converting job lead — but only if you follow up. Most freshers forget. Most referrals die in WhatsApp threads, never acted on.
🤖
ATS Blindness
Resume parsing systems reject qualified candidates before a human reads anything. Freshers don't know which keywords to include — or that this problem even exists.
Root Cause

Freshers don't fail job searches
because they lack skills.
They fail because they lack a system.

08

What 5 User Interviews
Actually Revealed

Before writing a single line of PRD, I interviewed 5 freshers and early-career job seekers. The findings shifted my original assumptions significantly.

4/5
Were tracking jobs in WhatsApp chats or notes
Nobody used spreadsheets by default. Most relied on WhatsApp "Saved Messages" or phone notes — unstructured, unsearchable, and easy to lose track of.
3/5
Had missed at least one recruiter response in the last 3 months
Not because they were lazy — but because recruiter emails landed in Primary, Promotions, or plain got buried. The cost: a missed interview they were qualified for.
5/5
Had warm referrals they never followed up on
Every single person had at least one "I should follow up with that person" referral rotting in their contacts. The intent existed. The system to act on it didn't.

"I apply, then I forget. I only remember when I accidentally see the email again weeks later. By then it's too late."

User interview #3 — Fresh graduate, 6 months into job search
KEY PIVOT FROM RESEARCH

I originally assumed users wanted better job discovery — smarter recommendations, more platforms. What research revealed: they don't struggle to find jobs. They struggle to manage the ones they've already found. This reframed DreamJob from a discovery tool to an execution tool.

The Hard Calls:
What We Chose & Why

Every product decision is a tradeoff. Here are three calls I made — and the reasoning behind each one.

Option A — Rejected
ATS Resume Helper in MVP
High wow-factor. Users mention ATS confusion in research. Could be a strong acquisition hook for freshers who don't understand keyword matching.
VS
Option B — Chosen ✓
Tracking + Reminders + Email Sync First
Less glamorous, but solves the immediate daily pain. Users are losing opportunities right now due to missed follow-ups — not just ATS failures.
PM Reasoning
ATS help is a pre-application problem. Tracking and follow-ups are a during-application problem. If we don't fix the active bleeding first, users will churn before they ever see the ATS feature. Additionally, ATS parsing is technically complex — shipping it half-baked in MVP risks damaging trust. Build the core habit loop first, layer intelligence on top of retention.
Option A — Rejected
Mandatory Email Connect on Signup
Maximum data capture from day one. Email sync is our most powerful feature — forcing connection upfront ensures users get full value immediately.
VS
Option B — Chosen ✓
Email Connect Optional, Manual Mode First
Lower barrier to entry. Users can start tracking immediately without a privacy decision. Email sync introduced after they've experienced core value.
PM Reasoning
Research showed users are privacy-sensitive about email access — especially for a new, unknown app. Forcing this at signup would cause drop-off before users experience any value. The right pattern: earn trust first, ask for permissions later. Once users see DreamJob saving them real opportunities, they'll be far more willing to connect email. Activation trumps data maximalism at this stage.
Option A — Rejected
Web App (Desktop-First)
Easier to build, more screen real estate for dashboards and analytics. Most PM tools today are web-first products.
VS
Option B — Chosen ✓
Mobile-First
Indian freshers live on their phones. Job applications happen on the go — on buses, between classes, during lunch. Desktop is secondary.
PM Reasoning
India has 700M+ smartphone users and mobile-first internet behaviour. Our target users — tier-2/3 college students and early-career professionals — are far more likely to add a job application from their phone than open a laptop. The follow-up reminder use case is also inherently mobile: you need a push notification, not a browser tab. Desktop can come later; mobile is where adoption happens.
